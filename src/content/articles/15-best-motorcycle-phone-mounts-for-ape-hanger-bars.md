## Why Phone Mounts for Ape Hanger Bars Are Different  

Ape hanger bars aren’t your classic 1-inch handlebars — they often measure 1¼ to 1⅜ inches or more. This means a typical phone mount designed for standard bars might fall short or slip under the weight or stress of bouncing vibrations. The taller position of ape hangers also demands a mount that keeps your phone visible but not in the way of your hand movements or controls.

Since I’ve spent hours prepping my own Vicla-inspired bagger with chrome big ape bars, I learned the hard way: clamps need to be rugged and adjustable. Rubber cushioning is a must to avoid scratching your bars or letting the mount shift around. And when riding low and slow, nobody wants to fiddle with a phone holder in the middle of a traffic stop or cruise night.

## Features to Look for in Ape Hanger Bar Phone Mounts

When I picked the right mounts for my builds, I prioritized these features:

- **Adjustable clamp diameter**: Ensure the mount clamps firmly around at least 1¼ inches and up to 1⅜ or more.  
- **Vibration isolation**: Look for silicone pads, spring-loaded grips, or shock-absorbing materials to protect your phone and keep it readable.  
- **360-degree rotation and tilt**: Easy to angle your phone in portrait or landscape without dismounting.  
- **Strong yet soft grips**: Silicone or rubber grips hold your phone snugly but won’t mar the case or screen.  
- **Weather resistance**: Rain or dust, your mount should keep your phone shielded or offer quick removal.

## Installation and Setup Tips for Ape Hanger Mounts

When mounting on ape hangers, these are my go-to tips:

- **Prep the bars:** Clean and dry the clamp area thoroughly. I keep a microfiber and alcohol wipe handy in my garage for this.  
- **Protect your chrome:** Use rubber or silicone pads that come with the mount to avoid scratching your meticulously polished bars.  
- **Tighten securely but smartly:** Too tight can damage bars or stress the mount; too loose means your phone shakes loose mid-ride. Always check clamp torque with the tools recommended by the mount brand.  
- **Adjust positioning:** Make sure the mount doesn’t interfere with hand controls or cables. I prefer to place it near the center or on the riser, where I can glance down without taking my eyes off the road for too long.  
- **Test vibrancy and grip:** Before hitting the road, bounce gently and check if the phone remains rock solid.

## Caring For Your Phone Mount and Phone on the Road

Over time, your bike’s vibration, weather, and road debris mean your mount needs maintenance:

- **Regular retightening:** I check clamps every few rides to keep grip solid.  
- **Clean silicone grips:** Dirt and oil can reduce grip. I use mild soap and water to wipe them down without degrading the material.  
- **Check for UV damage:** Leaving mounts in direct sun for extended periods can weaken plastics or rubber. Store your bike inside or cover it when not riding.  
- **Add a rain cover:** Some mounts offer detachable rain shields, perfect for coastal rides or passing clouds.  
- **Charge your phone safely:** Consider hardwire kits or rechargeable batteries to keep GPS and music humming without cable tangles.

## FAQ  

### What size clamp do I need for ape hanger bars?  
Ape hangers usually come in sizes from 1¼ inch to 1⅜ inch in diameter. Choose a phone mount with an adjustable clamp diameter that covers this range for a secure fit.

### Can I use a regular motorcycle phone mount on ape hanger bars?  
Most regular mounts fit standard 1-inch handlebars and might slip or not tighten correctly on taller ape hangers. Look for mounts labeled universal with wider clamp ranges or those designed specifically for large-diameter bars.

### How do I prevent my phone from vibrating loose on rough rides?  
Opt for mounts with vibration dampening features such as silicone grips, spring-loaded holders, or shock absorbers. Regularly checking and tightening your mount helps keep your phone secure.

### Is it better to mount the phone on the riser or the bar grip section of ape hangers?  
Mounting near the riser often provides better visibility and less hand interference. However, choose a spot that won’t obstruct controls or cables and feels comfortable for your riding style.

### Can I charge my phone while it’s mounted?  
Some mounts like Rokform and Quad Lock support compatible charging accessories or have wiring kits. Otherwise, you’ll need a separate handlebar USB charger or portable power bank.
